About Me

From chemical reactions to neural networks to investment decisions — a non-linear journey through technology and capital.

The Journey

I started my career in Chemical Engineering, fascinated by how molecular structures determine material properties. That same pattern-recognition mindset led me to machine learning, where I discovered that neural networks and chemical reactions share surprising parallels — both are about transformations, optimization, and finding elegant solutions to complex problems.

Over 3.5 years as an ML Engineer, I built systems that processed millions of data points daily. I worked on image processing for national intelligence, developed AI healthcare applications that assisted radiologists, and at Capital One, created fraud detection systems protecting millions of customers in real-time.

The technical work was deeply fulfilling, but I found myself increasingly curious about the business side — why certain ML companies succeeded while others failed, how to identify genuine innovation versus hype, and where capital could create the most impact.

This curiosity led me to pursue an MBA, where I studied finance, strategy, and entrepreneurship. The transition to Venture Capital was natural — I could combine my technical depth with investment analysis to back founders building the next generation of AI and ML companies.

Today, I invest in early-stage startups at the intersection of machine learning and real-world applications — healthcare, fintech, enterprise automation. My engineering background helps me evaluate technical feasibility and team capabilities, while my business training guides investment thesis and market analysis.

I also run a consulting practice helping small businesses leverage machine learning. Many companies know they need AI but don't know where to start. I help them identify high-impact use cases, build proof-of-concepts, and deploy production systems that drive real ROI.
