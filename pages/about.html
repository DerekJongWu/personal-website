About Me

From neural networks to investment decisions — a non-linear journey through technology and capital.

The Journey

I spent 3.5 years as an ML Engineer building systems that processed millions of data points daily. I worked on image processing for national intelligence, published research on the first iterations of generative AI, and at Capital One, created fraud detection systems protecting millions of customers in real-time.

In parallel to my full-time work, I ran a consulting practice helping small businesses leverage machine learning. Many companies knew they needed AI but didn't know where to start. I helped them identify high-impact use cases, built proof-of-concepts, and deployed production systems that drove real ROI. This side work gave me invaluable perspective on how different organizations approach technology adoption and where friction points emerge.

The technical work was deeply fulfilling, but I found myself increasingly curious about the business side — why certain ML companies succeeded while others failed, how to identify genuine innovation versus hype, and where capital could create the most impact.

This curiosity led me to pursue an MBA, where I studied finance, strategy, and entrepreneurship. The transition to Venture Capital was natural — I could combine my technical depth with investment analysis to back founders building the next generation of AI and ML companies.

Today, I invest in early-stage startups at the intersection of machine learning and real-world applications — fintech, cybersecurity, enterprise automation, and quantum technologies. My engineering background helps me evaluate technical feasibility and team capabilities, while my business training guides investment thesis and market analysis.
